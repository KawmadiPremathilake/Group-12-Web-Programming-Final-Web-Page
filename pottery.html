Pottery Industry

The main material that is used in Sri Lankan pottery industry is clay. There are main three types of clay that are used for pottery. As a result of the large scale of use, clay has become a scarce resource in Sri Lanka. Mainly clay is available in the areas of Nattandiya, Dediyawela, Boralasgamuwa, and Meetiyagoda.

Sri lankan clay products

Sri Lankan potters create unique products using pottery equipment, such as water-storing vessels like Kalagediya and Gurulettuwa. These vessels absorb and remove contaminants from drinking water, and some are still used today. Clay-based cooking pots are also important in the local kitchen. Terracotta, a fired clay, is used in various products, including tiles, bricks, and decorative ornaments. Terracotta tiles are highly water-absorbing and suitable for indoor flooring and outdoor settings.

Roof tiles, made from the same ingredient, are excellent heat absorbers and can last over 100 years if treated right. However, the use of asbestos roofing sheets has been banned in most countries, highlighting the health benefits of Terracotta roof tiles.

The Pottery Making Process

Pottery in Sri Lanka is an SME industry and livelihood for certain communities. Villages like Wadakkarawewa produce artistic pottery by collecting clay during the dry season, seasoning it, and removing dirt. Traditional methods, such as sakaporuwa, are indigenously developed to meet modern customer expectations. This combination of aesthetics and functionality creates artistic clayware.
